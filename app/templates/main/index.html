What is Wake-on-LAN?

Wake-on-LAN (WoL) is a network standard that allows a computer to be turned on remotely by a network message. The message is usually sent by a program executed on another device on the same local area network.

It works by sending a specially formatted network packet called a "magic packet" to the target device's MAC address, which signals the network interface to power on the system.

How Our App Helps

Our Wake-on-LAN application makes it easy to:

  • Store and manage multiple devices with custom names
  • Wake up devices with a single click from anywhere
  • Track wake-up history and success rates
  • Organize devices by purpose or location
  • Schedule automatic wake-ups at specified times

Getting Started

To use Wake-on-LAN, your devices must have WoL enabled in their BIOS/UEFI settings and network adapters must be configured properly. Our application streamlines the process of sending the magic packets, but your network and devices need to be WoL-compatible.
